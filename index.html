Roads & Highways

Roads are routes or ways on land for transportation, typically surfaced with asphalt, concrete, or gravel. Highways are major roads intended for high-speed travel over long distances, often connecting cities or regions. They typically have multiple lanes, controlled access, and higher design standards compared to ordinary roads. Expressways and freeways are divided highways designed for high-speed travel with limited access. They typically feature grade-separated interchanges, controlled entrances and exits, and barriers separating opposing traffic. Rural roads connect rural areas, agricultural lands, and smaller towns. They may vary in design and surface type depending on traffic volume and local conditions.
